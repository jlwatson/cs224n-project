

I agree with mh-- if there are real compatibility issues or weaknesses, then
those would be a good reason to switch curves.  
  
If there aren't, sep256k1 seems plenty good enough for the forseeable future.
I think most programmers (myself included) have a tendency to worry about
small-probability "what if" problems that we know how to solve, when we'd be
better off thinking about high-probability problems that we don't want to
think about because we don't already know how to solve them.  
  
Like multifactor wallet authentication so trojans don't steal users'
wallets....
