Historical note: According to [Prisoner's Dilemma By William Poundstone](http://books.google.com/books?id=JZk2x841s2AC&amp;pg=PA4&amp;lpg=PA4&amp;dq=von+neumann+soviet+union+nuclear&amp;source=bl&amp;ots=C_nPcP7Lpo&amp;sig=0FqKuOehUsHxJ3FLTeQiMt13ftc&amp;hl=en&amp;sa=X&amp;ei=3vCzU_zbDo-nyATT1oGwCg&amp;ved=0CB4Q6AEwADgK#v=onepage&amp;q=von%20neumann%20soviet%20union%20nuclear&amp;f=false), von Neumann didn't suggest issuing a nuclear ultimatum but instead advocated a surprise first strike against the Soviet Union. Bertrand Russell did suggest a nuclear ultimatum but with the goal of establishing a world government rather than just non-proliferation.

In a [previous related discussion](http://lesswrong.com/lw/14a/thomas_c_schellings_strategy_of_conflict/zun), I noted that I I Rabi and Enrico Fermi did propose using the threat of nuclear attack to deter the development of fusion weapons. However in my online searches, I haven't found any prominent historical figures suggesting the exact thing that you (and I in that previous thread) are suggesting here, of using a nuclear threat just to prevent the proliferation of fission weapons, which is kind of curious...
